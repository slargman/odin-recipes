Toast, but from France

  • Prep: 10 mins
  • Cook: 20 mins
  • Total: 30 mins
  • Servings: 12
  • Yield: 12 slices

Description

This is a recipe that dates back to Marie Antoinette. Legend has it that when she would light up a fat doobie and get the munchies she'd bang around in the palace kitchens trying to make some food. Being the Empress of Europe, however, she had no idea what she was doing and accidentally knocked flour, eggs, and sugar into a bowl and accidentally mixed them until combined. Then, tripping over her bodice she inadvertently sliced some bread and dipped it in the mixture, before flinging it into the air to land on the well-oiled palace griddle. When poor Marie managed to pick herself off the floor she found a perfect French toast waiting for her. At this point Joan of Arc, who was doing her seventeenth unpaid internshp as a scullery wench while trying to break into politics, arrived to see what the commotion was all about. She found a contented Marie munching on her toast, pausing to utter her famous remark: “Let them eat pancakey, for I will be eating all the French toasty.”

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 3 eggs
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon white sugar
  • 12 thick slices bread

Steps

  1. Measure flour into a large mixing bowl. Slowly whisk in the milk. Whisk in the salt, eggs, cinnamon, vanilla extract and sugar until smooth.
  2. Heat a lightly oiled griddle or frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Soak bread slices in mixture until saturated. Cook bread on each side until golden brown. Serve hot.
